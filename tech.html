MARS

(Mechanized Autonomous Rail Scanner)

SpaceX has expressed a need for a rail and sub-track visual observation system by which an operator could identify abnormalities or foreign object debris (FOD) on the track, and we get to build it! However, we've decided to go a few steps further with our designs. Our imaging system will consist of standard "action cameras" to stream information to controllers on site, and we are writing image processing code to perform basic edge detection and object recognition in order to aid the observer in identification of FOD. Not only this, but we've also made sure to include room for expansions such as laser scanners or our aforementioned ITIC system.

In order to run the scanner system, a robotic transportation system is needed to shuttle our imaging solution down the rail. The Mechanized Autonomous Robotic Scanner or “MARS” is designed to be an especially stable and reliable shuttle for which the imaging system will be housed.

We suppose this qualifies for working on a MARS mission with SpaceX, so we plan to pad our resumes accordingly.




ITIC

(Intra-Tube Inspection of Condition)

This system deals with detecting abnormalities present on/in the walls of the tube, such as dents, cracks, deformed seams, and oxidation. Unlike other inspection technologies, this will be accomplished without the need to change the normal operation of the entire hyperloop system, despite its high speeds. This system intends to aid in the maintenance of the tube. When data from the system's secondary motion tracking capabilities is integrated with the tube condition data, the severity of defects can be estimated, and repairs can be prioritized.

The main goal in our design of this system is to make the process of maintaining the tube as streamlined and non-interruptive as possible. Current inspection technologies would require the Hyperloop to shut down for hours or even days, which would result in huge financial losses. We hope to avoid this with our primary tube inspection system using an entirely imaging-based method. Current designs make it incredibly small, and therefore compatible with virtually any pod design. By using existing capture technology, this system will feature off-the-shelf components making this system efficient, easily implemented, and most importantly cost effective.

UPDATE:

-This technology will be exhibited on RUMDLoop's pod at Competition Weekend 2016.
-It’s also likely that this tech will be used on a later iteration of the MARS rail scanner.



ITOC

(Intra-Tube Optical Communications)

ITOC is a communication system designed specifically for Hyperloop which would augment or supplant any wireless communication system currently being planned.We hope to design and build a system that can significantly outperform WiFi in both range and bandwidth and with data rates in excess 1Gb/s, such that passengers can enjoy high speed, latency free internet during their commute.

Hyperloop is going to be known for pioneering this state-of-the-art transportation system. Our ITOC system can further add to the list of ‘firsts’ Hyperloop will accomplish. With increasing demands for high-speed data, it will be ever more pertinent to provide modern high-bandwidth communication methods to keep up with this demand. ITOC will be able to meet and exceed nearly every consumer requirement, making it robust and giving Hyperloop a unique competitive edge over other transportation systems.
